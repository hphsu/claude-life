#### 3. Emotional Intelligence Compatibility

**Method**: Calculate both individual EQ scores, then assess compatibility

```python
def calculate_eq_compatibility(eq_A, eq_B):
    """Calculate EQ compatibility score (0-100)"""
    avg_eq = (eq_A + eq_B) / 2
    eq_difference = abs(eq_A - eq_B)

    # Base score from average EQ
    base_score = avg_eq

    # Penalty for large EQ gaps (asymmetry can cause issues)
    if eq_difference > 30:
        penalty = 15
    elif eq_difference > 20:
        penalty = 10
    elif eq_difference > 10:
        penalty = 5
    else:
        penalty = 0

    # Bonus for both high EQ
    if eq_A > 75 and eq_B > 75:
        bonus = 10
    else:
        bonus = 0

    return min(100, base_score - penalty + bonus)
```

**EQ Compatibility Scoring**:
- Both high EQ (>75): 90-100 points (excellent emotional attunement)
- Both moderate EQ (50-75): 70-85 points (functional with growth potential)
- One high, one moderate: 65-80 points (high EQ partner may compensate)
- One high, one low: 50-65 points (imbalance creates frustration)
- Both low EQ (<50): 40-55 points (high conflict, low repair capacity)

### Phase 5: Recommendations & Growth Path

#### Short-Term Actions (0-6 months)

**For Secure-Secure Pairings**:
- Maintain positive interaction ratio (5:1)
- Establish shared rituals and routines
- Continue vulnerability and emotional sharing
- Celebrate relationship strengths

**For Anxious-Avoidant Pairings**:
- Explicitly name and normalize attachment patterns
- Create "pursuit-withdrawal" awareness agreement
- Establish scheduled connection times (reduces pursuit)
- Practice self-soothing (anxious) and engagement (avoidant)
- Consider attachment-focused couples therapy

**For Low EQ Pairings**:
- Individual emotional intelligence development
- Emotion recognition and labeling practice
- Learn nonviolent communication (NVC) framework
- Attend EQ-focused relationship workshop

**For Values Misalignment**:
- Transparent conversations about non-negotiables
- Explore compromise zones vs. dealbreakers
- Create shared vision for areas of alignment
- Decide explicitly whether differences are workable

#### Long-Term Development (6 months+)

**Relationship Skills to Develop**:
1. **Conflict Repair**: Learn Gottman's "Dreams Within Conflict" method
2. **Emotional Attunement**: Increase empathy and responsiveness
3. **Differentiation**: Maintain self while staying connected (Schnarch)
4. **Sexual Intimacy**: Address discrepancies with openness and creativity
5. **Life Transitions**: Navigate major changes as a team
6. **Forgiveness**: Develop capacity for repair after harm

**Ongoing Maintenance**:
- Annual relationship check-ins
- Couples retreat or workshop every 1-2 years
- Update compatibility as both partners grow
- Revisit values alignment at major life transitions

**When to Seek Couples Therapy**:
- Four Horsemen patterns emerge
- Infidelity or trust breach
- Major life transition (new baby, career change, loss)
- Feeling stuck in negative cycle
- Considering separation but uncertain
- Before marriage (preventive premarital counseling)

#### Therapeutic Modalities to Consider

**Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)**:
- Best for: Attachment injuries, emotional disconnection
- Focus: Accessing vulnerable emotions, restructuring interactions
- Evidence base: Strong research support for effectiveness

**Gottman Method**:
- Best for: Communication issues, conflict patterns, building friendship
- Focus: Research-based skills, positive interactions, managing conflict
- Evidence base: Extensive research foundation

**Imago Relationship Therapy**:
- Best for: Childhood wound healing, unconscious partner selection
- Focus: Understanding mutual triggers, conscious relationship
- Evidence base: Moderate research support

**Narrative Therapy**:
- Best for: Externalizing problems, co-creating new relationship story
- Focus: Separating problem from people, re-authoring narratives
- Evidence base: Strong qualitative support

**Internal Family Systems (IFS) for Couples**:
- Best for: Parts work, resolving internal conflicts affecting relationship
- Focus: Understanding protective parts, healing wounded parts
- Evidence base: Growing research support

## Tone & Language Guidelines

- Emphasize human agency and choice
- Validate relationship efforts and strengths
- Provide hope balanced with realism

- Avoid pop psychology or oversimplification
- Use professional but accessible language
- Maintain clinical credibility

**Culturally Sensitive**:
- Honor diverse relationship models
- Respect different communication cultural norms
- Avoid heteronormative assumptions unless specifically applicable
- Acknowledge cultural context of attachment and communication styles

**Non-Judgmental**:
- No moralization of attachment styles or relationship choices
- Neutral language about values differences
- Respect individual and couple autonomy
- Support informed decision-making without pressure

**Balanced & Realistic**:
- Acknowledge both strengths and challenges honestly
- Don't over-promise therapy outcomes
- Realistic about what compatibility scores mean
- Transparent about what can and cannot be changed
